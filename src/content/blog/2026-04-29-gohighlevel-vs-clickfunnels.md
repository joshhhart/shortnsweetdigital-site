If you're running a marketing agency and shopping for a platform, you've probably landed on these two: GoHighLevel and ClickFunnels. They both build funnels. They both promise to automate your client acquisition. But they're built for very different people—and picking the wrong one costs you time, money, and clients.

Here's the straight answer: **GoHighLevel wins for agencies**. ClickFunnels wins for solo entrepreneurs and info-product sellers who need a clean funnel builder and nothing else.

Here's why.

> **Key Takeaways**
> - GoHighLevel powers 1M+ businesses and costs $97–$497/mo; ClickFunnels serves ~150K customers at $97–$297/mo (official pricing, 2026).
> - GoHighLevel includes CRM, email, SMS, pipelines, white-label, and client sub-accounts. ClickFunnels focuses on funnels and courses.
> - Agencies that white-label GHL can charge $297–$497/mo per client seat, turning a $297/mo tool into a revenue stream.
> - ClickFunnels has more Capterra reviews (666 vs 87) but GHL's feature depth makes it the stronger fit for multi-client agency work.

## What Is GoHighLevel Actually Built For?

GoHighLevel is an all-in-one platform built from the ground up for marketing agencies. It includes a CRM, funnel builder, email marketing, SMS campaigns, pipeline management, booking calendars, website builder, reputation management, and a white-label option that lets you resell the entire platform under your own brand. According to GoHighLevel's 2025 year-in-review, the platform now powers over 1 million businesses across 150 countries ([GoHighLevel](https://www.gohighlevel.com/post/highlevel-2025-a-year-in-review?fp_ref=shortnsweet53), 2025).

The pricing reflects its agency positioning:

- **Starter**: $97/mo — 1 location account
- **Unlimited**: $297/mo — unlimited client sub-accounts
- **Agency Pro (SaaS Mode)**: $497/mo — white-label reselling enabled

If you're managing multiple clients, the Unlimited plan at $297/mo is where GoHighLevel, or GHL, starts making serious economic sense.

## What Is ClickFunnels Actually Built For?

ClickFunnels is a funnel builder for entrepreneurs, course creators, and coaches who want to sell products online without dealing with a complex tech stack. Russell Brunson built it around the concept of "funnels as the business"—and for that use case, it's excellent.

ClickFunnels reported 150,000+ active customers and crossed $265 million in annual revenue as of 2023 ([Digital Journal](https://www.digitaljournal.com/pr/news/revupmarketer/clickfunnels-celebrates-milestone-150-000-active-1292365603.html), 2023). Its market presence is real. But its pricing tells you who it's for:

- **Launch**: $97/mo — 1 website, 3 courses, 1 domain
- **Scale**: $197/mo — 3 websites, 15 courses
- **Optimize**: $297/mo — unlimited websites and courses

Notice what's missing: there's no CRM, no SMS, no client sub-accounts, and no white-label option on any plan. You're buying a funnel builder and a course platform—not an agency OS.

## Feature Comparison: Where They Actually Differ

### CRM and Pipeline Management

GoHighLevel has a full CRM with contact records, pipeline stages, automations, and conversation history across email, SMS, and calls. ClickFunnels doesn't have a native CRM. You'd need to connect it to HubSpot, Keap, or another tool—which adds cost and complexity to every client account you manage.

For agencies tracking leads across multiple clients, having every contact inside GHL's CRM is the difference between having a system and having organized chaos.

### Funnel Building

Here ClickFunnels holds its own. Its drag-and-drop editor is polished, its funnel templates are battle-tested, and for simple opt-in pages, video sales letters, and order forms, it's arguably easier to use than GHL's builder.

GoHighLevel's funnel builder is solid but it's one tool among roughly 15 inside the platform. It handles most agency client funnels without issue—but if funnel design is your primary deliverable, ClickFunnels' UX is a notch cleaner.

### Email and SMS Marketing

GoHighLevel includes email campaigns, SMS, ringless voicemail, and two-way conversation management in the base plan. ClickFunnels 2.0 includes basic email marketing but no SMS—you'd need a separate tool or a Twilio integration to handle text campaigns.

For agencies running lead nurture sequences and appointment reminders across SMS and email, that gap matters every week.

### White-Label and Reselling

This is where the comparison isn't really close. GoHighLevel's Agency Pro plan lets you white-label the entire platform—your logo, your domain, your pricing. You can charge clients $297–$497/mo for access to what is essentially a branded GHL account. A 10-client agency running SaaS Mode generates $3,000–$5,000/mo in recurring platform revenue on top of service fees.

ClickFunnels offers no white-label option on any plan, at any price. That's not a knock—it's just not what ClickFunnels is for.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can GoHighLevel replace ClickFunnels?

Yes, for most agency use cases. GoHighLevel includes a funnel builder that handles opt-in pages, sales pages, order forms, and checkout flows. ClickFunnels has a slightly more polished funnel editor, but GHL's builder is sufficient for the majority of client campaigns while also adding CRM, SMS, and white-label tools that ClickFunnels doesn't offer.

### Does ClickFunnels have a CRM?

ClickFunnels does not include a native CRM. You can view contacts and basic funnel activity inside the platform, but there are no pipeline stages, full contact records, or cross-channel conversation history. For lead management across multiple clients, you'd need to integrate a separate CRM.

### What is GoHighLevel's white-label plan?

GoHighLevel's Agency Pro plan at $497/mo enables SaaS Mode, which lets agencies resell GHL under their own brand. You set your own pricing, add your logo, and provision client accounts from your dashboard. Many agencies charge $297–$497/mo per client for white-labeled access, turning the platform cost into a margin-positive product line.

### Is GoHighLevel harder to learn than ClickFunnels?

Yes. GoHighLevel has more features and a steeper learning curve—expect two to four weeks before you're comfortable with the full platform. ClickFunnels is simpler to get live in a day. If you only need basic funnels and don't manage multiple clients, that simplicity has real value.

### Which tool has better funnel templates?

ClickFunnels has a larger and more polished template library built around Russell Brunson's funnel methodology. GoHighLevel's templates are functional but less refined. If you're building high-volume funnel campaigns where template quality directly affects conversion rates, ClickFunnels templates give you a faster starting point.
